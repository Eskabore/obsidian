Mansa Musa

The Gold's Emperor

Tribute to Africa Wealthiest Man

He was not only famous in Africa, the Middle-East and beyond, for his incommensurable wealth, but also for distributing his wealth to poors he would meet along the road. He spend and gave so much gold to people during his stay in the Egyptian's capital of Cairo, that gold price underwent a noticeable drop for over a decade.

  • ~ 1280 Born in Mali

  • 1311-1312 Became King of Mali

  • 1324 Starts pilgrimage to Mecca

  • 1325 Mansa returns from Mecca with scholars & architects

  • 1337 Death of Mansa Musa

  • 1339 Appeared on the Map of the World in Europe

"Musa was 'the most powerful, the richest, the most fortunate, the most feared by his enemies and the most able to do good to those around him.""

-- Al-Omari (North African Scholar), Source: Davidson, Basil. The African Past: Chronicles from Antiquity to Modern Times.
